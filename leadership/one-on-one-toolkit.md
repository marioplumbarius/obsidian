# Tech Lead 1:1 Toolkit

> Your output is now your team's output. The 1:1 is your highest-leverage half hour to raise that number.
> **Default posture:** they own the agenda, you listen ~80%, you remove obstacles, you resist solving.

---

## Part 1 — Pre-1:1 Checklist (pull this up before each meeting)

### Before (5 min)
- [ ] Skim their section of the running doc — what did *they* add?
- [ ] Review last meeting's action items (yours and theirs)
- [ ] Glance at recent work / PRs / tickets so you have context
- [ ] Note **one specific, genuine piece of recognition** (not "good job" — name the thing)
- [ ] Check: am I about to run a status update? If so, kill it and move status async.

### During (30 min)
- [ ] Open with **"What's on your mind?"** → then stay quiet
- [ ] Their agenda items first, yours second
- [ ] When they bring a problem, ask before answering:
      - *"What would you do if it were entirely your call?"*
      - *"Where exactly do you feel stuck?"*
- [ ] Give the recognition you prepared
- [ ] Surface any feedback (small, specific, timely — don't save it for reviews)
- [ ] Close the trust loop: **"What could I be doing differently to support you better?"**
- [ ] Agree on action items out loud, with an owner for each

### After (3 min)
- [ ] Log action items in the running doc
- [ ] **Do your half this week.** A 1:1 where your commitments never close is worse than none.

### Anti-patterns to catch yourself doing
- Talking more than ~30% of the time
- Solving the problem instead of growing the person (→ you become the bottleneck)
- Cancelling silently (reschedule instead — cancelling says "you don't matter")
- Only ever talking shipping/status, never career or energy

## Topic rotation (so it never decays into status)
Cycle through these — don't hit all every week:

| Cadence | Topic | Sample opener |
|---|---|---|
| Most weeks | Blockers / obstacle removal | "What's slowing you down that I can clear?" |
| Most weeks | Are we working on the right thing? | "If you owned the roadmap, what would you cut?" |
| Every 2–3 wks | Feedback (both directions) | "What's one thing I should keep doing / stop doing?" |
| Every 3–4 wks | Career & growth | "Picture yourself in two years — what are you doing?" |
| Ongoing | Energy / sustainability | "What's energizing vs. draining you right now?" |
| Ongoing | Wins & recognition | (you bring it — see prep checklist) |
| Occasionally | Skip-level / team dynamics | "What would you want leadership to know?" |

## Coaching questions (keep in your back pocket)
- "What would you do if you were me?"
- "What does 'done well' look like for this?"
- "What's the smallest next step?"
- "What are you most / least sure about here?"

*References behind this: Grove, *High Output Management* (the 1:1 belongs to the report); Google Project Oxygen ("good coach" is the #1 manager behavior); Wiseman, *Multipliers* (give ownership, ask don't tell — avoid the ex-star-IC bottleneck trap).*
