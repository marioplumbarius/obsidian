# 22 Career Advice Bits from David Anderson

## Summary
David Anderson (ex-Amazon Tech Director and GM) shares 22 loosely-ordered thoughts on tech careers and leadership. The visible pieces emphasize emotional intelligence, simplification, the value of communication skills, understanding process WHY, and prioritizing tech exposure early in your career.

## Key advice (visible)

1. **Emotional intelligence drives success** — knowing when to question senior leaders vs stay quiet, reading people, anticipating what matters to important stakeholders. A significant portion of career success comes from this skill.

2. **80% of solutions are overbuilt** — complex projects usually have a much simpler solution that delivers 90% of the value at a fraction of the maintenance cost.

3. **Communication skills = technical skills** — engineers with great communication tend to get promoted faster because they work on visible, cross-team projects. They're rarer and equally valuable.

4. **Understand the WHY, not the nitty-gritty** — Agile, Waterfall, Scrum, Kanban all have tradeoffs. Understanding the philosophy matters more than ritual adherence. Example: sprint velocity vs estimates is the linchpin of successful agile.

5. **Early career: prioritize tech exposure over income** — Learn popular, industry-wide technologies. Engineers stuck on internal-only tech have serious long-term career problems when they change jobs.
